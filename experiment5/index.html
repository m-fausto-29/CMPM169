Experiment 5 - 3D Graphics

Imitate

For this experiment, I decided to replicate the goal ring that appears at the end of a level in the video game series Sonic the Hedgehog. The idea came to me as I was browsing through 3D art pieces in OpenProcessing and I came across an example that showcased a 3D ring.

Integrate

After looking through various examples online from sources such as the lecture slides, YouTube, the p5.js library, Open Processing and by simply searching through Google I was able to find a few examples that I could use as a starting point. I then integrated the code into my own project and began to modify it to fit my needs.

Innovate

One of the topics that caught my attention from the previous experiment was the use of a webcam, so I thought of integrating that into one of my existing revolving objects as a texture. I also thought of adding a particle system that I previously used to the ring to make it look more interesting. Finally, to make the ring closely resemble the reference photo even further, I made use of what I learned about 3D shapes to create the result I wanted to finish off the piece.

Reflection

With the help of a few resources from online, I worked on this piece mainly by myself and learned a lot through this process. For the most part, it was a matter of trial and error when figuring out how to integrate the pieces of code the way I wanted to. The main high I would say about working on this piece would mainly be the freedom to recreate a piece of nostalgia from my childhood. While the piece may not be reflected well in my static webpage in comparison to what I have in my p5.js editor, the time and effort is still the same.
